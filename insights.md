## What This Dataset Is

This dataset answers a question most Melbourne businesses can't answer confidently: *what does it actually cost to acquire a customer in my industry, and what is that customer worth over time?*

It covers 18 industry verticals — from plumbing to legal services, pet care to financial advisory — with real figures for advertising costs, conversion rates, returns on ad spend, and customer lifetime value. All figures are in Australian dollars and drawn from sources including WordStream, Australia Post, CBRE, the Australian Dental Association, and Salt & Fuessel's own published client campaigns.

Plain English version of the key terms used throughout:

- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition):** how much you pay in advertising to win one new customer or lead
- **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend):** for every $1 spent on ads, how many dollars come back in revenue
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** the total revenue one customer generates over their entire relationship with your business — not just their first purchase
- **CVR (Conversion Rate):** the percentage of people who click your ad and then actually do what you want them to do (call, book, buy)

## 5 Key Findings

### 1. The most expensive leads are often the best value — if you know the full picture

Legal Services has the highest cost to acquire a client in Melbourne — between **$200 and $800 per lead**. At first glance, that sounds alarming. But Legal also has the highest return on ad spend of any industry in this dataset at **4.21x** — meaning every $1 spent on advertising returns $4.21 in revenue.

Why? Because a single legal client can be worth $50,000 to $500,000 in fees over a relationship. When you understand what a customer is worth over time, a $500 lead cost looks very different.

**The takeaway for Melbourne businesses:** Never judge your advertising cost without comparing it to your customer's lifetime value. A high cost per lead in a high-value industry is not a problem — it is a competitive advantage for businesses willing to invest.

---

### 2. Most Melbourne businesses are optimising for the wrong number

The data reveals a dramatic gap between what a customer costs to acquire and what they are actually worth over time:

| Industry | Cost to Acquire (AUD) | Customer Lifetime Value (AUD) |
|---|---|---|
| Financial Advisory | $130–$800 | $29,175 |
| Automotive & Tyres | $35–$55 | $7,938 |
| Dental & Healthcare | $100–$165 | $2,297–$4,568 |
| Fashion eCommerce | $120–$200 | $324 |

A financial advisory firm with a $29,175 lifetime value per client can afford to spend $800 to acquire one and still return more than 36 times that investment. Yet many firms in this category cap their ad spend based on the cost of a single consultation — leaving enormous returns unrealised.

**The takeaway for Melbourne businesses:** Set your advertising budget based on lifetime value, not first transaction value. If you do not know your customer's lifetime value, calculating it is the most important thing you can do before your next campaign.

---

### 3. Pet services is Melbourne's most underrated digital marketing opportunity

Of all 18 industries in this dataset, Pet Services has the second-highest conversion rate at **13.07%** — meaning nearly 1 in 8 people who click a pet services ad become a lead. It also has one of the lowest costs to acquire a customer at approximately **$49**.

At the same time, 73% of Australian households own a pet, and the average dog owner spends **$3,300 per year** on their pet (Animal Medicines Australia, 2025). A loyal grooming customer who visits five times a year over six years is worth close to $3,000 to a single business.

Salt & Fuessel's work with Dogdayz — a multi-location dog care provider in Victoria — grew leads by **14.5% year on year** and social-driven leads by **77%**, supporting the launch of a fourth location.

**The takeaway for Melbourne businesses:** If you operate in pet services, boarding, grooming, or veterinary care, the numbers are strongly in your favour. High conversion rates and strong lifetime value make this one of the most cost-efficient verticals in Melbourne for digital advertising.

---

### 4. Automotive converts better than every other industry — by a wide margin

Automotive repair, servicing, and tyres achieved the **highest conversion rate of all 18 verticals in this dataset at 14.67%**. That means nearly 1 in 7 people who click an automotive ad book an appointment or make an enquiry.

The reason is intent. Someone searching for a tyre replacement or a brake service is not browsing — they need something done, often today. That urgency translates directly into conversions.

Combined with an average cost per acquisition of approximately **$44** and a customer lifetime value of around **$7,938**, automotive is one of the strongest performing verticals in Melbourne for digital advertising return.

**The takeaway for Melbourne businesses:** If you run a workshop, tyre shop, or automotive service centre, digital advertising is working harder for you than for almost any other industry in this city. The question is not whether to advertise — it is whether your campaigns are structured to capture that intent efficiently.

## What To Do With This Data

**If you are a business owner:** Find your industry in the dataset. Compare your current cost per lead against the benchmark range. Then calculate your customer lifetime value using the formula in the README. If your lifetime value is significantly higher than your acquisition cost, you are likely underinvesting in digital advertising.

**If you are a marketing manager:** Use the benchmark figures to set realistic performance targets for your campaigns and defend your budget internally. A CPA that looks high in isolation often looks very reasonable when benchmarked against your industry and your customer's lifetime value.

**If you are evaluating agencies:** Ask any agency you speak to what ROAS and cost per acquisition they have achieved for clients in your industry. Then compare their answer to the benchmarks in this dataset. The numbers do not lie.
